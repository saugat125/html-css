GREENHOUSE

How We Work

Khagraj Krishi greenhouse is located in Teraute Tole of Ghorahi Sub-metropolitan City in Lumbini Province. The greenhouse is climate-controlled using sophisticated modern technology, making it possible to maintain the optimal temperature and humidity for growing our produce.

Large fans and cooling pads lower the temperature, and a fogger machine increases the humidity during hot and dry months.

Outside and inside curtains prevent damage from the scorching sun during the summer and trap the heat inside during the winter.

A drip irrigation system brings a set amount of water and nutrition to each plant based on the stage of its growth and time of the year.

ORGANIC PRODUCT

How are the capsicums kept pesticide-free?

Khagraj Krishi greenhouse is located in Teraute Tole of Ghorahi Sub-metropolitan City in Lumbini Province. The greenhouse is climate-controlled using sophisticated modern technology, making it possible to maintain the optimal temperature and humidity for growing our produce.

Nets on the greenhouse ventilations prevent insects from entering. This, along with the controlled space of the greenhouse, alleviates the risk of airborne diseases from insects.

Although soil provides many of the necessary nutrients for plants, it is also home to harmful bacteria and fungus that attack plants. By cultivating our capsicum in cocopeat, Khagraj Krishi is eliminating the risk of soil-borne diseases.

As a result, we do not use any chemical pesticides. Instead, we spray “neem-jhol” once a week as a traditional, organic and sustainable alternative to chemical one.

INSPIRATION

Khagraj Lohani

Khagraj Krishi Udhyog Pvt. Ltd. is named after Khagraj Lohani, the grandfather of the founder.

Khagraj Lohani was a pioneering farmer in Dang, who introduced new farming techniques. He was the first to use a tractor, create artificial dams to collect irrigation water for winter crops by utilizing water pumps etc. He started a rice and oil mill to process local crops to directly serve his customers.

Apart from agriculture, Khagraj Lohani also shaped the development of his village and the district of Dang. Along with his friends and contemporaries, he was involved in establishing Rapti Medical Institution, the first hospital in the region. Similarly, the first college in Dang, Padmodaya Campus, the first primary school in the village, Bangaun School, and the road connecting the Dang Valley to Lamahi and further to the border town of Koilabaas all bear the legacy of his efforts to better his community.

Khagraj Lohanis greatest strength was his belief that we ourselves have to do the work necessary to realize our vision, without waiting for help from the government. He believed that when the people exercise their agency, they can build their collective dreams into reality.

Twenty five years since his retirement, and six years since he passed away, he is still remembered fondly by his community for his untiring vision, his tenacity in the face of adversities, and his resilience. At Khagraj Krishi, we strive to emulate his spirit and continue on the path of service, innovation and persistence which he has shown us through his work.
